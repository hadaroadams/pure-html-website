Descriptions on Banku

Banku is a traditional Ghanaian dish that is made from fermented corn and cassava dough. It is a popular staple food in Ghana and is often served with a variety of soups, stews, and sauces. To make banku, corn and cassava dough are mixed with water and left to ferment for several days. The mixture is then cooked on the stove, stirring constantly until it becomes thick and smooth. The resulting dough is then shaped into balls and served hot. Banku has a slightly sour taste due to the fermentation process and has a soft, doughy texture. It is often eaten with soup or stew made from fish, meat, or vegetables. Banku is also sometimes served with okra soup or groundnut soup, which are both popular in Ghanaian cuisine. Banku is a nutritious and filling meal that is rich in carbohydrates and fiber. It is a staple food in Ghanaian households and is enjoyed by people of all ages. If you're looking to experience traditional Ghanaian cuisine, banku is definitely a dish to try.
